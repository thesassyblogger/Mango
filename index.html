Origin story

The mango began as a tropical Asian legend.

4000+ years

Ancient Roots

Mangoes have been cultivated in South Asia for thousands of years, becoming part of food, medicine, poetry, gardens, and royal gifts.

Indo-Burma

Birthplace Belt

The cultivated mango is linked to the region around northeastern India, Bangladesh, Myanmar, and the wider Southeast Asian tropics.

Trade routes

World Traveler

Traders, sailors, and colonizers carried mangoes west to Africa, then onward to Brazil, the Caribbean, Mexico, and Florida.

Geography

Mangoes thrive where heat and rain know each other.

Mango trees love tropical and subtropical climates: warm winters, dry flowering seasons, monsoon rain, and long sunlit ripening days. That is why the mango map glows across Asia, Africa, Latin America, the Caribbean, Australia, and parts of the southern United States.

Who grows mangoes?

The mango world has many capitals.

India Largest producer; home to Alphonso, Kesar, Dasheri, Langra, Totapuri, and hundreds more.
Indonesia A major producer across humid islands, with mangoes woven into street markets and home gardens.
China Grows mangoes in warm southern regions such as Guangxi, Yunnan, Hainan, and Guangdong.
Thailand Famous for fragrant dessert mangoes, especially with sticky rice and coconut milk.
Mexico A leading exporter to North America, known for Ataulfo, Tommy Atkins, Kent, and Keitt.
Pakistan Celebrated for Chaunsa, Sindhri, Anwar Ratol, and a deep summer mango culture.
